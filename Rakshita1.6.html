Business Case 6: Public Safety Hotspot Mapping System

1. Business Case Description

Crowded public areas such as beaches, markets, and transport hubs can develop hidden danger zones due to overcrowding, repeated incidents, or suspicious activities.

The Public Safety Hotspot Mapping System analyzes past and current incident data to detect high-risk areas and alert authorities. Early detection enables effective deployment of security resources and ensures safer public environments.

2. Problem Statement

  • Repetitive danger zones
  • Incident clusters spread across nearby areas
  • High-risk regions needing immediate attention

System Requirements

  • Process large volumes of incident data
  • Detect clusters of unsafe areas
  • Rank hotspots by severity
  • Provide actionable insights in real time

3. Solution Approach

  • Hash Map stores incident records with instant lookup
  • K-Means clustering identifies groups of repeated incidents
  • Graph + BFS detects connected risky areas
  • Max-Heap ranks hotspots by severity

System Output

  • Real-time map of risk zones
  • Ranked hotspots by severity
  • Actionable alerts for authorities

4. Algorithms and Data Structures Used

K-Means Clustering — Identify Danger Clusters

Groups incident locations into clusters to reveal repeated unsafe activity.
Helps visualize where risk is concentrated.

Graph + BFS — Detect Connected Risky Zones

Creates connections between nearby unsafe points.
BFS finds larger continuous danger regions efficiently.

Hash Map — Fast Storage of Incident Records

Stores incident details (type, location, time) for instant access.
Enables quick updates and retrieval during analysis.

Max-Heap — Rank Highest-Risk Hotspots

Orders hotspots by frequency or severity of incidents.
Allows authorities to respond to the most dangerous zones first.

5. System Workflow

Start
↓
Record Incidents in Hash Map
↓
Apply K-Means Clustering
↓
Create Graph of Unsafe Points
↓
Detect Connected Risk Zones using BFS
↓
Rank Hotspots with Max-Heap
↓
Notify Authorities with Risk Map
↓
Deploy Safety Measures
↓
End

6. Space and Time Efficiency

Time Complexity

  • K-Means clustering: O(I × K × N × D)
  • BFS on graph: O(V + E)
  • Hash Map update/lookup: O(1) average
  • Max-Heap insertion/removal: O(log H)

Space Complexity

  • Hash Map storage: O(N)
  • Graph adjacency list: O(V + E)
  • Max-Heap storage: O(H)
  • K-Means centroids & cluster assignments: O(K + N)

7. Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) Alignment

  • SDG 3: Good Health and Well-Being

  • SDG 16: Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions

8. Conclusion

The Public Safety Hotspot Mapping System provides a data-driven solution for enhancing public safety. By combining clustering, graph traversal, and priority ranking, it identifies high-risk zones quickly and accurately, enabling authorities to respond efficiently and protect citizens.
