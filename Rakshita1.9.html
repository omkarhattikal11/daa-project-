Business Case 9: Marine Research & Conservation Analytics

1. Business Case Description

Coastal agencies in Velora rely on continuous ocean measurements—such as temperature, salinity, tide movements, and marine species behavior—to understand environmental changes.

The Marine Research & Conservation Analytics System processes large, noisy, and time-dependent data to identify ecological patterns and support long-term conservation planning. It helps researchers detect trends, anomalies, and vulnerable zones in marine ecosystems.

2. Problem Statement

  • Unpredictable ocean conditions
  • Incomplete or irregular logs
  • Sparse or inconsistent species sightings

System Requirements

  • Detect long-term environmental trends
  • Identify anomalies in marine habitats
  • Generate actionable insights for ecosystem protection

3. Solution Approach

  • Moving Average smooths noisy ocean data
  • Hash Map stores species sightings for instant retrieval
  • Graph models marine migration paths
  • DFS explores connectivity and isolated habitat zones

System Output

  • Smoothed trends of ocean parameters
  • Quick lookup for species data
  • Identified migration paths and isolated habitats
  • Insights for marine conservation planning

4. Algorithms and Data Structures Used

Moving Average (Time-Series Analysis) — Trend Smoothing

Removes daily fluctuations in ocean data such as temperature or tide levels.
Reveals clear long-term environmental trends.

Hash Map — Fast Species Data Lookup

Stores species sightings with keys like location or ID.
Allows instant retrieval from large datasets.

Graph (Migration Path Mapping) — Marine Movement Modeling

Represents animal routes as nodes and edges.
Helps study migration patterns and seasonal behaviors.

DFS (Depth-First Search) — Habitat Zone Exploration

Explores connected and isolated zones in the marine graph.
Identifies regions where biodiversity may be fragile or at risk.

6. Space and Time Efficiency

Time Complexity

  • Moving Average: O(N) per time-series
  • Hash Map lookup/update: O(1) average
  • Graph DFS: O(V + E), V = nodes, E = edges
  • Graph construction: O(E)

Space Complexity

  • Hash Map for species data: O(S), S = total sightings
  • Graph adjacency list: O(V + E)
  • Moving Average storage: O(N)

8. Conclusion

The Marine Research & Conservation Analytics System transforms raw ocean and species data into meaningful insights. By combining time-series smoothing, fast data storage, graph modeling, and zone exploration, it supports scientific decision-making and helps protect vulnerable marine ecosystems effectively.
