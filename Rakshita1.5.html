Business Case 5: Beachside Energy Usage Optimizer

1. Business Case Description

Beachside areas consume a large amount of electricity for streetlights, public lighting, shops, and utility services. However, much of this energy is wasted because lighting systems remain ON during low-crowd hours and do not adapt to actual usage demand.

The Beachside Energy Usage Optimizer analyzes real-time energy sensor data, identifies consumption patterns, and predicts low-demand periods. Based on this analysis, it automatically adjusts lighting and utilities, ensuring efficient energy usage, reduced costs, and improved sustainability.

2. Problem Statement

  • High electricity wastage during late-night or low-crowd hours
  • Uneven power distribution across beach zones
  • Manual monitoring and adjustment is inefficient at large scale

System Requirements

  • Monitor energy usage in real time
  • Identify high- and low-consumption zones quickly
  • Predict upcoming low-demand periods
  • Automatically optimize lighting and utility usage

3. Solution Approach

  • Queue manages incoming energy sensor readings
  • Hash Map stores the latest energy usage per beach zone
  • Segment Tree analyzes energy usage across time intervals
  • Min-Heap prioritizes zones with highest energy consumption

4. Algorithms and Data Structures Used

Segment Tree — Fast Energy Usage Analysis

Supports quick range-sum queries over time intervals.
Helps identify peak and off-peak energy usage periods efficiently.

Min-Heap — Prioritizing High-Energy Zones

Orders beach zones based on energy consumption.
Allows the system to optimize or dim the highest-consuming zones first.

Hash Map — Storing Latest Sensor Readings

Maps each zone to its current energy usage value.
Provides O(1) average-time access for live monitoring.

Queue — Managing Real-Time Sensor Updates

Processes incoming energy readings in arrival order.
Ensures smooth and timely system updates.

5. System Workflow

Start
↓
Sensors across beach zones send energy usage data continuously
↓
Readings are added to the Queue
↓
Hash Map updates the current energy usage per zone
↓
Segment Tree analyzes energy consumption over time
↓
Min-Heap ranks zones by energy usage
↓
System predicts low-demand periods
↓
Lighting and utilities are adjusted automatically
↓
Overall energy consumption is optimized
↓
End

6. Space and Time Efficiency

Time Complexity

  • Queue operations: O(1)
  • Hash Map update/lookup: O(1) average
  • Segment Tree query/update: O(log N)
  • Min-Heap insertion/removal: O(log Z)

Space Complexity

  • Queue storage: O(N)
  • Hash Map for zones: O(Z)
  • Segment Tree storage: O(4N) ≈ O(N)
  • Min-Heap storage: O(Z)

7. Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) Alignment

  • SDG 7: Affordable and Clean Energy
  • SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities
  • SDG 13: Climate Action

8. Conclusion

The Beachside Energy Usage Optimizer provides an intelligent and automated solution for managing electricity consumption in coastal areas. By using efficient data structures and predictive analysis, it reduces energy wastage, lowers operational costs, and supports environmentally sustainable beach management.
