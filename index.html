What is the course about?

This course teaches how to think like a problem-solver using algorithms. We learn how to build fast and correct solutions, understand why they work, and measure how efficient they are. The aim is to connect theory with real tasks like searching, routing, and handling data.

Data structures & algorithms studied

We explore many tools used in programming, such as arrays, lists, stacks, queues, different kinds of trees, tries, and graphs. We also learn hashing, sorting, searching, Dijkstra, dynamic programming, greedy ideas, and advanced structures like skip lists. Each one helps solve problems in its own way with different speed and memory needs.

Real-world connections

Algorithms are part of everyday technology — from GPS maps finding fast routes, to databases organizing information, to big online shops sorting products instantly. The course shows how these ideas are used in real life while also teaching the theory behind them.
